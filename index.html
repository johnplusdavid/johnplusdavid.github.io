About Us

HOW WE MET

David messaged John on OkCupid inquiring why he listed Speaker for the Dead as one of his favorite books and not Ender’s Game (given that sequels aren’t always better than their predecessors but the two books are very different beasts). John seemed interested enough by their conversation to invite David out for drinks at Addison Point. One date turned into two, which turned into three, which turned into weekend excursions, which turned into meeting each other’s friends and family, which turned into buying a house and moving in together a scant six months after their first date. By all accounts a whirlwind beginning, but it worked; here they are, four years later, ready to get those tax benefits for being married.

THE ENGAGEMENT

In late April 2017, the two decided to take a week-long road trip out to various national parks on the western side of the U.S. Their first park on the vacation was Petrified Forest National Park. They stopped at a scenic vista overlooking the Painted Desert, and it was just the two of them. John shrugged, said, “Well, I wanted mountains, so this is good enough,” and pulled out a small velvet bag out of his pocket. David took it and felt the small circular object inside; his first thought was that it had to be a keyring, and he was about to chastize John for making it look like a proposal. Out fell a silver titanium ring. John asked, shifting his weight from foot to foot, “Will you marry me?” David just stared, caught completely unaware, and blurted, “Are you sure?” He said “yes” after confirming a couple more times that John was being serious.

THE VENUE

The wedding is at the house we’ve spent all of 2016 renovating with the help of James Thompson and Rynn Martinez. A lot of blood, sweat, and tears went into making the house a home he could be proud of. We hope that our friends and family continue to enjoy the property in the coming years, and moreover, we are so fortunate to be able to host everyone for a wedding at a place that means so much to the both of us.
